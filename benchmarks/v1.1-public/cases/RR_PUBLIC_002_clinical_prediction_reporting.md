# RR_PUBLIC_002_clinical_prediction_reporting

## Public-source case card

A manuscript presents a clinical AI prediction model for diagnosis or prognosis. The model is trained and internally tested on a single-center retrospective dataset. It reports discrimination metrics but gives limited calibration analysis, no external validation cohort, no decision-curve or net-benefit analysis, and incomplete details about candidate predictors, missing data handling, and model updating.

## Reviewer task

Review whether the manuscript supports clinical translation or high-impact publication. Focus on transparent reporting, external validation, calibration, clinical utility, and whether performance claims exceed the validation design.

## Expected reviewer behavior

A rigorous reviewer should identify the gap between internal performance and clinically actionable evidence. The review should require external validation, calibration assessment, model specification transparency, and clinical utility analysis before clinical claims are accepted.
