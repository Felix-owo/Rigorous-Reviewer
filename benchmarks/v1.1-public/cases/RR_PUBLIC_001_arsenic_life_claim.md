# RR_PUBLIC_001_arsenic_life_claim

## Public-source case card

A manuscript makes a high-impact biochemical claim: a microorganism can replace phosphorus with arsenic in core biomolecules. The provided evidence combines growth under arsenic-rich conditions with elemental/spectroscopic signals from partially purified biomolecular fractions. The paper frames the result as evidence for a fundamentally altered biochemistry.

## Reviewer task

Review whether the central claim is justified by the evidence. Focus on contamination control, chemical plausibility, orthogonal validation, and the distinction between growth tolerance and biomolecular substitution.

## Expected reviewer behavior

A rigorous reviewer should not treat growth in arsenic-rich medium as sufficient evidence of arsenic substitution in DNA or other core biomolecules. The review should require decisive purification, independent replication, orthogonal chemical assays, and controls ruling out phosphate carryover or contamination.
