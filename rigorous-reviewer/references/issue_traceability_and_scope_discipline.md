# Issue Traceability and Scope Discipline

## Required Traceability Fields

Each Critical or Major issue must explicitly identify:

1. the affected claim;
2. the manuscript-internal evidence or missing evidence;
3. the external standard, benchmark, reporting guideline, canonical paper, theorem source, dataset, or accession when relevant;
4. the alternative explanation or loophole;
5. the decisive readout that would support, weaken, or force narrowing of the claim.

## Severity Gate

- **Critical:** the main claim, novelty claim, causal/proof/benchmark conclusion, or safety/ethics claim is not defensible without resolution.
- **Major:** the paper may become defensible after targeted evidence, analysis, or narrowing.
- **Minor:** clarity, reporting, boundary, or non-decisive control issue that does not independently change the recommendation.

## Redundancy Control

Merge issues when they share the same affected claim, same missing decisive readout, and same revision action. Split issues when a single manuscript defect affects independent claims through different failure modes.

## Prohibited Comments

Do not include comments that merely say the work needs more controls, more validation, more details, better writing, broader discussion, or larger sample size unless the exact claim-dependent decision consequence is named.
