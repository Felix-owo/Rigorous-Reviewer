# Evidence Audit and Source Policy

## Evidence Classes

- **Manuscript-internal:** figures, tables, methods, source data, supplements, proofs, algorithms, code, datasets, clinical protocol, accession IDs.
- **External primary:** peer-reviewed papers, official standards, canonical methods, benchmark papers, trial registries, database records, theorem sources.
- **External secondary:** reviews, perspectives, preprints, tutorials, vendor or tool documentation.
- **User-supplied local:** lab notes, unpublished data, reviewer comments, private protocols, internal SOPs.

## Rules

1. Do not mix evidence classes without labeling them.
2. Do not invent citations or identifiers.
3. If a source is needed but unavailable, mark it as a missing source rather than fabricating support.
4. Source strength must match claim strength. A causal or clinical claim requires stronger support than a descriptive claim.
5. External anchors calibrate standards; they do not replace manuscript-internal evidence.
