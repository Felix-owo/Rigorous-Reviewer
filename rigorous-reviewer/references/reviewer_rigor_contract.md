# Reviewer Rigor Contract

## Non-Negotiable Standard

A rigorous review is not a summary plus suggestions. It is a decision-oriented stress test of the central claims.

Each Critical or Major issue must answer:

1. What exact claim is threatened?
2. What exact evidence is missing, ambiguous, confounded, or invalid?
3. Why does the gap matter for the conclusion rather than merely for presentation?
4. What alternative explanation remains open?
5. What experiment, analysis, proof step, benchmark, source-data release, or validation would resolve it?
6. Would resolution change the recommendation?

## Severity Calibration

- **Critical:** A central claim likely fails, is unsupported, is unsafe, is mathematically invalid, or cannot be interpreted without new decisive evidence.
- **Major:** A central or important supporting claim is weakened and requires substantial additional evidence, analysis, or correction.
- **Minor:** Reporting, clarity, limited-control, or presentation problem that does not change central interpretation if fixed.
- **Optimization:** Useful improvement that is not required for the decision.

## Red Lines

Reject or require major revision when any central claim depends on:

- unvalidated causal inference;
- missing essential control;
- unresolved confounder;
- untraceable sample/data provenance;
- circular benchmarking;
- leakage or invalid baseline;
- proof gap that invalidates the theorem;
- claims beyond assay resolution;
- reproducibility impossible from reported methods.
